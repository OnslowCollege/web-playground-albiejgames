Roots in Papua New Guinea

I was born in a small village in Papua New Guinea. My early life was simple: limited electricity, no high-end gear, and only bits of the outside world coming in through a small radio or the rare trip to town.

But that environment shaped me. It taught me resourcefulness. It taught me creativity. It taught me to dream.

Challenges I Faced

Getting into content creation wasn’t easy. My internet was often unstable, my equipment was basic, and many around me didn’t understand why I was doing this. But I believed in a vision: telling stories, connecting with people, and building something bigger than my surroundings.

I spent countless hours learning video editing, practising with what I had, and using free tools when I could. There were days when progress felt impossible—but those were the days I doubled down.

The Turning Point

2018

First real attempts at video: shaky, raw, but honest.

2019

Got feedback from early viewers. Started learning how to edit.

2020

Upgraded to slightly better gear. Optimized upload workflow.

2021

First 1,000 subscribers—proof that what I was doing mattered.

2023

Aspired to reach audiences beyond PNG, dreaming of global impact.

Moments That Defined Me

Through photos, I share moments of learning, of hardship, and of joy. From the first video camera to milestone celebrations, each frame tells a part of the story.

Looking Forward

My goal is to become the world’s biggest YouTuber. Not just in subscribers, but in heart: to connect with people, to build community, and to inspire. I know it’s a huge dream — but what’s life without big dreams?

With every upload, I try to improve. With every setback, I try to learn. And most importantly, I keep believing that where I come from is not a limit — it's the foundation.
